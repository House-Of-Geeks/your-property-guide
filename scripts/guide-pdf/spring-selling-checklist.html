First, pick your listing weekend

Spring campaigns are won in winter. Photography happens a fortnight before launch, styling and trades before that, and your agent and conveyancer before everything. Choose the weekend you want your campaign live, then start the countdown on the matching Monday.

Start the checklist week ofCampaign goes liveThe window
Monday 27 July 2026 11–12 September Early spring. You list while most vendors are still painting fences, so your home meets the season's first buyers with the least competition.
Monday 24 August 2026 9–10 October Peak spring. The biggest buyer audience of the year, and the most competing stock. Presentation and pricing discipline decide who wins October.
Monday 21 September 2026 6–7 November The last full window. Auction or exchange lands late November, before the market winds down for Christmas. In Victoria, mind the Melbourne Cup pause (Tuesday 3 November).
Already past your start date?

The 12 weeks are comfortable, not mandatory. To compress to eight, run weeks 12 and 11 together (research while you interview agents), decide on styling in the same week you book trades, and hold the launch sequence — the last six weeks — exactly as written. What you should never compress: contract preparation. In NSW, Victoria, Queensland and the ACT your paperwork must be ready before marketing starts, and a late contract is the most common reason launch dates slip.

The countdown

Weeks count down to exchange, with settlement following two to six weeks later. Chapter references point into your copy of The Complete Guide to Selling Your Property in Australia.

WhenWhat to doDone
Phase one · Groundwork weeks 12 to 9
Week 12 Research your suburb: pull six months of comparable sold prices, days on market and current competition, and build your own price range (Chapter 2). Book a free appraisal at yourpropertyguide.com.au/appraisal so your plan starts from evidence. Start decluttering, one room a week. ☐
Week 11 Shortlist and interview three agents using the 10 questions (Chapter 5). Ask each for a written appraisal with comparables, a marketing plan with line-item costs, and their commission including GST. Phone their past vendors. ☐
Week 10 Appoint your agent; negotiate commission and the agreement term before signing. Engage your conveyancer or solicitor and start contract or disclosure preparation (mandatory before marketing in NSW, VIC, QLD and the ACT). Notify your lender and ask about discharge timing and any fixed-rate break costs. ☐
Week 9 Book trades for repairs and painting — spring is their busy season, so lock dates now. Order a pre-sale building and pest report if you've decided to (Chapter 4); in the ACT, commission the mandatory report pack. Fix every small broken thing. ☐
Phase two · Presentation weeks 8 to 7
Week 8 Decide on styling: consultation, partial or full staging — stylists book out fast ahead of spring, so get two quotes this week. Finish decluttering and deep-clean. Tackle street appeal: garden, fence, letterbox, driveway. Spring buyers see your garden at its best; make sure it is. ☐
Week 7 Trades and styling complete. Book photography for the property's best light, plus floorplan and (if warranted) video. Confirm the contract or disclosure pack is ready. Agree the launch price and the advertised price strategy in writing. ☐
Phase three · Launch weeks 6 to 5
Week 6 Approve photos, floorplan and copy (check every fact yourself). Sign off the portal tier and total marketing spend. Listing goes live late in the week to catch the weekend's saved-search alerts. Signboard up. ☐
Week 5 First open homes. Demand written feedback after each: groups through, repeat visitors, contract or disclosure requests, price feedback. This fortnight is your biggest audience; treat every enquiry as a live one. ☐
Phase four · Campaign and close weeks 4 to settlement
Week 4 Second weekend of opens. Review the campaign data against launch expectations. Strong traffic, no offers? Hold briefly. Weak traffic? Reprice decisively now, not in week 2. Auction campaigns: review buyer interest with the agent and start thinking about reserve. ☐
Week 3 Offers arriving (or auction week approaching). Insist on written offers with all conditions. Assess each on price, conditions, deposit and settlement terms (Chapter 8). Keep your pre-set floor in front of you. ☐
Week 2 Negotiate and accept, or auction day: set the reserve from campaign evidence the same week. Contracts signed or exchanged. Buyer's cooling-off period runs (state table, Chapter 8). Deposit paid into the agent's trust account. ☐
Week 1 Buyer's conditions in play: finance approval and building and pest inspection. Respond to any renegotiation attempt with your own evidence, not panic. Lodge your mortgage discharge authority with your lender immediately. ☐
Settlement window
(2 to 6 weeks later)		 Conveyancer prepares the transfer; lender processes the discharge (chase weekly). Book removalists, redirect mail, arrange utility disconnections for settlement day, keep insurance current until settlement. Leave the property in contract condition with all included items in place. ☐
Settlement day Buyer's pre-settlement inspection passes. Settlement completes electronically; mortgage paid out, balance lands in your account, agent releases keys and remits the deposit less commission. Keep the settlement statement with your tax records. Done. ☐
The three spring bottlenecks

Everything on this list is quick on its own; three things book out from August and blow up timelines when left late: trades (week 9), stylists (week 8) and contract preparation (week 10). Lock those three early and the rest of the countdown looks after itself.
